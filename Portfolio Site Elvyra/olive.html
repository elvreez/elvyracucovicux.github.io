Olive

UX/UI Design / Research / Wireframing / Prototyping 2023

Background

I completed this project for my CareerFoundry UX design course. The prompt was to design a responsive application that users can store health information with.

Goals

Create an intutive responsive mobile app where users can log and view their health data, plus set goals and reminders to achieve good physical and mental wellbeing.

Requirements

  • onboarding page
  • navigable menu
  • store and log medical information
  • log in and sign up

User Research

During the research part of this project, I conducted a competitive analysis and devised a list of questions to ask participants to understand the goals, challenges, behaviors, and frustrations of users.
I selected five participants who fit various demographic categories for the user interviews and analyzed the responses to define key features needed for the app:

Streamlined Health Tracking

Insight: Users do not consider mental and physical wellbeing mutually exclusive

High Level Personalization

Insight: Users have the need for personalied health metrics beyong weight and calorie consumption when managing their health.

Features that Motivate Users

Insight: Users need help staying motivated to continue app use.

Educational Components

Insight: Users like to have additional information to learn and motivate themselves and track their progress.

Notifications and Reminders

Insight: Users need guidance, like notifications and reminders, to help
hem stay on track.

User Flows

After developing user personas, I created various user flows based on the needs, goals, and motivations of my personas. Creating user flows helps identify the process a user would go through to complete their desired task, and eventually helps composite a site map.

Design Language

While working through Olive's UX design through the iterative process, I began to define the UI elements of the app and created a style guide and pattern library:

Color

Olive's primary color is a bluish-green, both colors associated with wellness and calm. Much of how color is assigned is based on guidelines of Material Design, but uses a custom palette.



UI Elements




Typography

All typography uses poppins font. Use black over teal, light teal, and light green.
Use white over other colors. Colored text is used on some titles and forms.
